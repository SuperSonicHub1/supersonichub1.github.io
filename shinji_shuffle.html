An Analysis of "Shinji Ikari Learns The Melbourne Shuffle" by MSTRMND

https://soundcloud.com/urbec/shinji-ikari-learns-the-melbourne-shuffle

Unlike many other tracks by MSTRMND, "Shinji Ikari Learns The Melbourne Shuffle", which from now on will simply be referred to as "Shuffle," makes use of themes of industrial music, the basis of the track making use of harsh sounds. The artist themselves the track as "something a bit different." It is the fifth and final track on their mixtape The WAVE Files Vol. 2; the other tracks in the tape are by comparison much more light-hearted in tone.

Cover Art (and Title)

https://i1.sndcdn.com/artworks-aRfI0Mq59oRW711y-P1VxBQ-original.jpg

As much as I have searched to find the origins of the cover art of "Shuffle," I have been unsuccessful. The image depicts Misato Katsuragi, a main character of the Evangelion franchise. She: is wearing a yellow tank top has her back facing the "camera" head turned to the left tongue sticking out the left corner of her mouth eyes partially closed.

Interestingly, this last track is the only one in the tape to feature an anime character that isn't Winry Rockbell of Fullmetal Alchemist as the cover art; this further makes this track feel like the odd one out.

Speaking of Evangelion, the title of the song references another Evangelion main character, Shinji Ikari, with whom Misato has a strong relationship.

Finally, the track title also references the Melbourne shuffle, which Wikipedia describes as "a rave dance that developed in the 1980s."
